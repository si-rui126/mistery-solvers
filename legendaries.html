Legendaries

Can Rifts give themselves completely to their Mythos? If so, meeting one face-to-face would be a life-altering, identity-shattering experience.

- City of Mist Player's Guide, p30

Legendaries have chosen to not hold back but instead to revel in their Mythos. These individuals become truly powerful, channeling the fantastical qualities of their Mythos without fear or doubt. Legendaries still have a persona and attachments they care about beyond the sheer agenda of their Mythos, but these are often kept secret or are sidelined because they in fact limit their Mythos.

Mythos: King Midas

Aurmes is the CEO of the fashion brand Aurmes. He uses his overflowing wealth to maximized his company's success. He lost his son to brain cancer three years ago, but since becoming Awakened and learning the powers of the Mythos running around the City, he is on a mission to find someone to bring his son back. With his money and resources, it isn't hard to find someone desperate enough for the task.

Mythos: Daedalus

Dallas is an architect contracted by Mistapolis Penitentiary. He was tasked with creating a wing of the prison strong enough to contain the warden's son, Ryan. He was motivated by winning freedom for his son, Carl, who was a prisoner at the penitentiary. After many blueprints and the construction of an elaborate maze in a cavern underneath the prison, he realized that he did not have what it takes to trap Ryan. He made Carl trick Lenny O'Connor into entering Ryan's maze to distract him, while Dallas and Carl made their way out. Dallas had engineered two sets of wings made from pillow feathers, gum, and his Rift magic, with which he and his son attempted to escape from the prison with. Dallas's fate is currently unknown.

Mythos: Unknown

Fortuna is the owner of West Town Casino. She is rather aloof and proud, but she is willing to listen to reason. Lenny O'Connor owes her a large sum of money after using his money-disappearing trick during his time at the casino. Fortuna does not forgive debts easily, and she is not afraid to use more nefarious means to ensure that a debt is paid.

Mythos: Prometheus

Dr. Talbot Leyland is the founder of Helix Labs, a pioneer in Rift-tech and healthcare. He believes his fire will warm up the City, and indeed one of his greatest aspirations is to revolutionize the City into a futuristic tomorrowland where problems are solved scientifically, starting with the fields closest to his heart: healthcare, life sciences, and medicine. With his success, he funds his older brother Scott Leyland's "startup".

Mythos: The Boy Who Cried Wolf

A trendsetter, an uninhibited news pundit, a fashion icon, a diva -- Monte Wolfe (The Boy Who Cried Wolf) is all of those things and more. He's flashy and extravagant just as much as he's witty and charming, and knows exactly how to use his irresistible presence and charisma through all channels of the media to exert influence and sway public opinion to satisfy his mythic need: to cause alarm to as many people as possible.

Mythos: Minotaur

Ryan grew up with violent tendencies, resulting in his father locking him up from a young age. He killed his babysitter when he was only two years old. His father put him in the depths of Mistapolis Penitentiary, giving him only prisoners to eat. Due to the isolation and neglect, Ryan holds a lot of pent-up rage and resentment to not only his father, but also to anyone who stands in his way. He is very strong, as he is able to crash through walls with his charging move.
